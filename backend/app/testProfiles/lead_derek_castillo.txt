Derek Castillo is a Senior Sales Representative at VaultEdge Cyber based in Denver Colorado where he has been working since 2020. Before VaultEdge he was at a cloud infrastructure startup in Boulder for about two years selling SaaS solutions to mid-market companies in the financial services and healthcare industries. He has roughly 10 years of experience in B2B technology sales primarily focused on cybersecurity and cloud solutions. He graduated from Colorado State University in 2016 with a degree in Information Systems and a minor in Marketing. His LinkedIn headline says he helps companies build enterprise grade security without enterprise budgets and he describes his sales philosophy as solving problems not pushing products. He is a huge Denver Broncos and Denver Nuggets fan and has had Nuggets season tickets for the past few years. On weekends he plays in a recreational soccer league in the Denver area and also enjoys hiking around Rocky Mountain National Park with his dog. He volunteers as a mentor with the Denver chapter of SCORE helping small business owners with cybersecurity strategy. He recently attended the SaaStr Annual conference in the Bay Area and posted about trends in vertical SaaS for compliance heavy industries. He is also active in the Denver Metro Chamber of Commerce and attends their networking events regularly. VaultEdge Cyber has around 120 employees and focuses on endpoint security and cloud security solutions for mid-market companies in compliance heavy industries like banking and healthcare across the western United States. Derek was also recently featured in a CyberScoop article discussing the growing demand for affordable cybersecurity solutions among small and medium sized businesses. In the article he talked about how many mid-market companies are stuck between consumer grade security tools that are not robust enough and enterprise solutions that are too expensive and complex for their IT teams to manage. He said that VaultEdge fills that gap by providing endpoint protection and threat detection that can be deployed and managed without a dedicated security operations center. He mentioned that the biggest objection he hears from prospects is not about the technology but about the perceived complexity of switching from their current security setup. His approach to overcoming this is to offer a 30-day pilot program where VaultEdge runs alongside the existing security stack so the client can see the value without any disruption. He has closed several major accounts using this strategy including a logistics company with 800 employees and a regional credit union with 12 branches across Colorado and Wyoming. On LinkedIn Derek regularly shares content about cybersecurity trends and has written several original posts about zero trust architecture and why traditional perimeter-based security models are no longer sufficient. He has also shared posts about the importance of security awareness training for employees and has recommended several platforms that offer phishing simulation tools.
